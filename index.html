Elifnaz Önder

Quantum Devices • Superconducting Circuits • Materials Research

I work on the intersection of condensed matter physics and quantum information, specifically superconducting qubits. Below are some highlights.

Single-Film Transmon Using NbN
(Yale; Michel Devoret Lab (QuLab))

Single film transmon qubits utilizing the superconductor - insulator transition of NbN.

High-Frequency Readout of Superconducting Qubits
(Yale; Michel Devoret Lab (QuLab))

Designed and measurement a 20 GHz readout cavity detuned from a 2 GHz transmon.

Al/AlOx/Al Josephson Junctions
(MIT; William Oliver Lab (EQUS))

Development of a novel fabrication method for Al:AlOx:Al Josephson junctions and characterized them.

Superconductivity in Ga:Ge Films for Vertical Josephson junctions
(NYU; Javad Shabani Lab)

Exploration of superconductivity on gallium-doped germanium films.

Monolayer WTe₂ Films
(Yale; Judy Cha Lab)

Fabrication methods and transport properties of monolayer tungsten ditelluride.
