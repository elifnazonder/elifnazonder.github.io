About Me

Elifnaz Önder

I graduated from Yale University in 2024 with a double major in Applied Physics and Mechanical Engineering & Materials Science. During my undergraduate years, I pursued interdisciplinary research in materials science, condensed matter theory and superconducting qubits.

After graduation, I continued as a postbaccalaureate researcher in the Devoret Qulab at Yale’s Applied Physics Department, focusing on investigating the superconductor-to-insulator transition in ultra-thin NbN platforms to fabricate superconducting qubits. I also work on detuned readout of superconducting qubits at high frequencies.

I currently work as an Associate Quantum Engineer at Quantum Circuits Inc. (QCI) in New Haven, where I contribute to the development of scalable quantum computing architectures.
